I Am
Tim Berners-Lee

I am the founder of W.W.W (World Wide Web)

Hello!

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist who is best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He was born in London, England in 1955 and grew up in a family with a strong technical background. He graduated from Oxford University in 1976 with a degree in physics. After graduation, Berners-Lee worked as an engineer and software developer for various companies before joining CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, in 1980. While working at CERN, Berners-Lee had the idea for a global hypertext system that would allow people to access information from anywhere in the world. He wrote the first proposal for the World Wide Web in 1989 and released the first website in 1991. Over the years, Berners-Lee has been a strong advocate for the open and democratic nature of the web and has fought to keep it free from censorship and commercial control. Berners-Lee has received numerous awards and honors for his work, including the Millennium Technology Prize, the Prince of Asturias Award for Technical and Scientific Research, and the Japan Prize. He is also a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and is the founder of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), an international organization that develops and maintains web standards.
